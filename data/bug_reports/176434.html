Comment 8 Ricardo Balduino 2007-04-30 14:25:06 EDT 
Reviewed, please address these comments and let me know, so I can verify.

- Brief description shouldn't call out the artifact name. By convention we write "This artifact does ...".

- In the main description:
   - "It's a critical artifact used to help make architectural decisions and explain those decisions to developers". My question is if it is really used to 'make' decisions or to 'capture' decisions made that will drive development?
   - typo: contraints
   - I think the following sentence is long and should be broken in two so it is easier to read: "At a minimum, it should list decisions and contraints the developers must follow in building the system, the justifications for those decisions and constraints, and describe the Architectural Mechanisms and where they should be applied."
   - The wording used sounds like this artifact imposes constraints in the 'way people work'. I think the point to be emphasized is that it captures design constraints and decisions, which should 'guide' the way the system is developed, or how the team can organize itself to develop the system in parallel, and things like that. It may just be a matter of how we word it, but agile teams may feel it too restricting.
   - communited?

- In the Representation Options
   - The he ...
   - I'd encourage us to list the less formal representation option first and list other more formal options next. Whoever reads the text for the first time may get the wrong impression about how formal artifacts in OpenUP are (that added to the fact this artifact has 4 templates...). Besides reordering the bullets, the paragraph describing the informal representation could be expanded. I think the whole point is to let the team decide which level of communication is required and choose the appropriate format.

- Overall, the artifact does not discuss the existence and importance of an executable architecture, which is fundamental to distinguish a document-centric process from an agile, prove-with-code type of process. There is only a link to the concept, but that does not catch the reader's attention to this important distinction.
