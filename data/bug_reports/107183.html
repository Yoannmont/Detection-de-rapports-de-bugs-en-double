Description Yves Harms 2005-08-17 04:13:32 EDT 
It seems the spam and worm community has finally found bugzilla.
We are getting spam and worm emails. This is really annyoing.

Is it possible to restrict access to bugzilla to registered users ?
This way we would at least prevent crawlers to get our email adresses from
bugzilla. 
You could create a guest account without bug posting rights fo people just
wanting to read the bugs.
Comment 1 Gunnar Wagenknecht 2005-08-17 07:13:28 EDT 
-1

Everything should be easy accessible without login restricitons. If you restrict
access search engines like Google won't be able to index the sites.

IMHO restricting access doesn't block address collectors if there is a "guest"
account. It's like locking your door and putting a note on it that explains
where the key can be found.

Span should be filtered on your mailserver. I also don't think that address
munging is a good solution as long as you really cut the domain name. I think
address crawlers might be able to detect some common munging schemes.
Comment 2 Yves Harms 2005-08-17 07:52:56 EDT 
My assumption is, that this isn't specifically targetet at Eclipse's bugzilla. I
think it is just a spider crawling the web and finding eclipse.org by chance. It
would be easy to block such spiders by restricting access to the site.

Sure, the guest account would need to have a special login (provided at the
startpage for readonly users). 

Would it be possible to completly hide the email adresses and replace it with
usernames ? E.g. adding a username as cc instead of an email ?

"Span should be filtered on your mailserver." 
In reality filtering can never be perfect, and would constantly requires extra
efforts by our staff. (You always need to check if you possibly filtered away
something important). 

Today for eclipse developers bugzilla is the prefered way for communication.
I think it is more important to protect this than to have bugzilla beeing
indexed by google.

Just take look into the newsgroups, many users nowadays dont use a working email
adress to prevent beeing spammed. If spam caused by the open availability of
email adresses at bugzilla continous to grow at some point people will start to
use similiar aproaches there.
Comment 3 Eclipse Webmaster 2005-08-17 09:29:00 EDT 
-1 for blocking guest access

Although I do agree that the e-mail address munging that Bugzilla currently
employs is very weak.  I am watching a bug on bugzilla.org for this.
Comment 4 Yves Harms 2005-08-17 09:56:13 EDT 
What about this idea ?
1. Activate http-autentification for all bugzilla-pages at your webserver
2. Create a single account for that autentification with login/password
bugzilla/bugzilla
3. Create a autentification-free gateway-page and post that username/password there.
Now everybody can still browse bugzilla without needing to register and the
crawlers are kept out. 
The Eclise main page would link to the gateway page and users failing to
autentificate theirself should be redirected to that page (can be configured in
apache somewhere).

No changes to bugzilla itself are required this way.

(In reply to comment #2)
> Sure, the guest account would need to have a special login (provided at the
> startpage for readonly users). 

That's pretty useless. Soon spiders will be able to use logins posted somewhere
on the website. It's also useless to block the access now if Bugzilla has
already been scanned because they now do have the email addresses of most users.

> Would it be possible to completly hide the email adresses and replace it with
> usernames ? E.g. adding a username as cc instead of an email ?

Actually, the email address _IS_ the username in Bugzilla. Thus, It's not possible.

> "Span should be filtered on your mailserver." 
> In reality filtering can never be perfect, and would constantly requires extra
> efforts by our staff. (You always need to check if you possibly filtered away
> something important). 

Actually, it depends on your admin and your setup. For example, if you don't
delete at server level but reject at SMTP time the sender will get a failure
note. If it was an important mail I'm pretty sure they will contact you again.

> Just take look into the newsgroups, many users nowadays dont use a working email
> adress to prevent beeing spammed. 

Well, only those users that think that this will work. Spiders are intelligent
enough to detect modified email addresses. And people will suffer from a loss of
connectivity. It's not possible to reply to them personally.

> If spam caused by the open availability of
> email adresses at bugzilla continous to grow at some point people will start to
> use similiar aproaches there.

Mhm. That won't work. If you use a dummy mail address ... how do people get a
password ... how will they be informed about assignments? Uhh, interesting,
isn't it?

Thats plain easy. Just delete the mailaccount after you got the passsword. There

Comment 7 Eclipse Webmaster 2005-08-17 10:42:29 EDT 
Yves, I feel your pain.  This is not an easy fix.  It's difficult to balance
being Open and having a private life.

Take it from someone whose e-mail address is plastered everywhere on this site,
unmangled.  I often get over 1300 e-mails a day (most of them bounces and
mailman complaining about stuff), but only about 30 SPAMs actually make it to my
inbox per day.  Of those 30, perhaps 15 get filtered by Thunderbird
automatically, leaving me to contend with 15 SPAMs, which I add to Thunderbird's
Junk filter.

*sigh* The root of the problem, in reality, is that SMTP sucks.
