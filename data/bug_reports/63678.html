Comment 2 Pat McCarthy 2004-06-02 10:51:11 EDT 
I'll post a guess but I'm interested in the official answer.

The Marker Views (Tasks/Problems) collect information about changes (resource 
change events) and start a process of determining how much has changed and how 
to update the view. In doing so they spin off jobs and UIJobs (or maybe 
WorkbenchJobs), to manage their reaction.

I see it as a job that finds the change, processes the change, and when enough 
work has been collected, starts a UIJob/WorkbenchJob to actually get the 
viewer to react.  This keeps the view close to current without making every 
single marker change a viewer refresh or update. 

At least that is my impression based on an observation/test cycle process I 
ran on the code a few months back.  Code has probably changed, but this is 
what my testing seemed to teach me (right or wrong - lets see what the owner 
of the code has to say to straighten out my tale).
