In an effort to get the Core tools working again, Jeff kindly volunteered to
head up this task. Work will be done in stages:

Comment 9 Dan Kehn 2004-04-29 16:22:24 EDT

Comment 10 DJ Houghton 2004-04-29 16:34:49 EDT 
Empty nodes means that someone asked for it. (they are lazily initialized) The
reason that the default has a child (mostly empty) for each bundle in the system
was a design decision. Since they are not persisted to disk, what are the
children of the "/default" node? I decided that they should be the bundles which
are installed on the system since any bundle can customize their preferences. 

With the "/instance" it was more straight forward...just see who has prefs
stored on disk.

The compatibility layer thing was just an artifact of the way I created the
plug-in. I've removed it and it still works ok.
