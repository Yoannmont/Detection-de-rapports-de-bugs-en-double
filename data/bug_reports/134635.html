Description Bob Duncan 2006-04-03 18:17:20 EDT 
The agent terminates incorrectly if it is interrupted when profiling with "Show execution statistics" (which prompts pre-aggregation of results at the agent side). 

a) If the agent is detached at any time, a SIGSEGV will occur when the application terminates (regardless of whether or not the agent was re-attached at the time of termination). 

b) If the agent is Paused and remains so at the time of termination, data is not sent but the application terminates cleanly. Pause followed by a later Resume causes no problem -ie: there is no data loss if the agent has been Resumed and remains so at the time of termination. 

These problems occur with and without data reloading (ie: regardless of the polling frequency).

Comment 13 Bob Duncan 2006-05-03 19:00:26 EDT 
As introduced initially, the problems were:

(a) SigSegv at normal application termination after detach.
    (i) after detach with no reattach 
    (ii) after detach followed by reaattach
(b) No data sent at pause or detach or after reattach
(c) No data sent when process terminates prematurely (e.g. via terminate toolbar button)

The underlying causes and solutions were as follows:

(a) Aggregation kept a record of threads separate from the master record kept by the base piAgent. The aggregation record was incomplete for threads started when the app was not being monitored (eg: at the start in enabled mode or after detach/reattach in enabled/controlled mode). Therefore the cleanup of resources at detach was only partial: the base cleanup was done but not the aggregation cleanup for unrecorded threads. This left dangling pointers in two separate contexts which accounted for the different segvs in (i) and (ii). The problems occurred most frequently in the enabled case but could potentially have occurred in the controlled case depending on the application and the creation of threads. An initial solution that was adequate for (i) was insufficient to fix (ii). The final solution was to register any threads started while the app was not being monitored by tying aggregation into loadStack().

(b) Data dumping at interruption of profiling was mis-placed (in case of detach) and incomplete (in case of Pause). The solution was to move the aggregation rollup and dump of data to suspendTracing().

(c) When process terminates prematurely due to things like the terminate button, the piAgent never receives control and is therefore unable to dump data or do anything else. This is inherent in the RAC protocol for such terminationas and it's impact is not new or limited to the aggregating feature. There is no "solution". 

These were diagnosed & fixed by 4/25. Further testing showed problems with the initial fix for (b):

(d) incorrect context due to orphan methods from loadStack processing after resume following a pause. The solution was to connect new call sequences to same call sequences recorded before pause.

(e) double counting of methods due to double "rollup" of aggregation data in stack entries after a pause. The solution was to invalidate that data after first roll-up.

These were diagnosed and fixed by 4/27.  A subsequent automation test of a pre-build of the  piAgent with fixes for (a-e) uncovered one non-critical bug 139537 which will be fixed separately.
