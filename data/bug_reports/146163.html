Description David Williams 2006-06-08 23:53:02 EDT 
I'll attach a gif image to highlight what I mean, but, 
*after* all callisto projects are installed, 
with them each contributing their own "update sites" for 
further discovery or updates, there is a lot of redundancy and, 
IMHO, some "sloppy" look to it due to inconsistencies. 

I doubt we can quickly and easily fix everything, but I think there is 
time to improve it some. So, in the following list, I'll call out specific 
teams I think need to respond to this bug, but .... anyone can chime in. 

Update Team: 
   There's two entries for "Graphical Editing Framework", but only see one 
   if I grep the installed features ... any idea what would cause two? 
   Also, if two are contributed, is the update manager smart enough to only present once? (I ask, since my recommendations below would result in some being 
contributed more than once). 

BIRT: 
   There's two entries for BIRT, one "BIRT", another "Business Intellegence and Reporting Tool (BIRT)". 
   One comes from you, the birt project, and one from me in the WTP project ... we list you as one of our "sister" discovery sites. 

   So ... would you be willing to change yours? I think acronyms should be spelled out (this is for "new users" after all). Agree? Can you change? 

TPTP: 
   Same situation as with BIRT. You provide acronym TPTP, I provide as sister site spelled out. Can/will you change yours? 

EMFT: 
   ok, who's sneaking that in :) ... I know the history, but ... imagine a new user ... even I have to scratch my head to figure out what EMFT could possibly stand for ... can you please spell out? 

ALL: 
   the use of "udpate site" in your site names seems way too redundant, with 
each other and the dialogs main title ... "Select update sites to visit while looking for new features". 

   I prefer to just use "Updates" ... as a compromise between "udpate sites" and 
"discovery sites" ... but, suggestions welcome. 


I've just realized too that several of us may have "translation schedule" considerations to keep in mind ... but, I'll enter this bug anyway, and perhaps we can improve the situation, or, at least, agree on a direction? 

Thanks everyone.
