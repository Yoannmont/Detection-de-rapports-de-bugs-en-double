Comment 14 Tom Hofmann 2005-07-22 05:23:32 EDT 
Alex, I have finally looked at the patch.

+ Everything is very close along the lines of the existing code
+ 'Collapse All' works as expected: everything (including top-level types) is
collapsed in the Java editor

Now, I just want to make sure I understand why this is really needed. To me the
 advantage of having actions like 'Collapse to defaults' or 'Collapse all XXX'
with XXX in {methods, comments, inner types} sticks out clearly (see comment 2
and bug 75354), but I have trouble seeing where 'Collapse all' helps me.

In the Java editor, when I 'collapse all', I don't have much left to see, ie. I
would prefer the top-level type not to be collapsed.

'Collapse All' may work well with other folding providers or in other document
types. -> I am tempted to release the text (non-jdt) part of this, as 'Collapse
All' makes sense as something the framework offers, but not for the Java editor.

I invite the voters and cc'ers of this bug to elaborate a bit on what they
expect from such an action.

Comment 23 Alex Weirig 2005-07-29 11:10:07 EDT 
Hi,

good to hear you like the idea about folding statements.

I also thought that once you introduce statement folding, you might get
overwhelmed with all the collapse/expand annotations. I had a talk with Kai-Uwe
Maetzel, and one of the ideas was to only show the annotations for the
collapseable section you are currently in. Of course, you would always see the
expand annotations (otherwise, who could you expand your collapsed code :-)

The other idea Kai-Uwe had, was to setup folding based on the AST. I think
that's a  nice idea because it could be a first step for a generic editor. Once
you have an AST for the language, you have folding support.

Alex
Comment 24 Allan Pratt 2005-07-29 13:11:34 EDT 
I agree that showing plus/minus icons for every statement would be cluttered. 
But on the other hand things like collapsing should be "discoverable." How do 
you know you can collapse a statement if you don't see anything in the UI? 
Where do you put your mouse and right-click?

What about this: we could leverage the existing control-click gesture, 
extending it for folding. Today, when you hold down control and move the mouse, 
identifiers are highlighted as links to show that you can click on them. This 
could be extended to open braces and close braces: the brace turns blue and 
acquires an underline, and you can click on it to collapse the block. If it's 
already collapsed, control-click will expand. This, in addition to the twisties 
in the left column.

Here is an additional thought: with so many more collapsible elements, what 
should "collapse all" really do? If I use "collapse all" and then expand a 
method, should I see the whole method, or will its statements all be collapsed? 
I think I'd like to see the whole method: otherwise it could get tedious. Maybe 
context menu items in any region should be "Expand/collapse all in this 
region." This might need some user testing, for usability and "minimum 
surprise."
