Comment 17 André Søreng 2007-10-26 17:37:55 EDT 
Looking at the JLS, there are at least two improvements that can
be made. One is highlighting suffixes on numbers. E.g. "1.5f", "148938L" and "4.3323D". The other is highlighting the period on floating points without a leading zero. E.g. ".3" (0.3). Anything more Pavel?
Comment 18 Pavel Rappo 2007-10-27 02:30:07 EDT

> leading zero. E.g. ".3" (0.3). Anything more Pavel?
> 

1. what about binary exponent "p"? (see JLS 3.10.2)

2. what about leading +/- sign? if it's placed in the beginning of number literal, it's not a part of it (just unary operator described in JLS 15.15.3 and 15.15.4), but if it's in the "exponent part", then it is a part of literal.
What I am talking here is that in both cases +/- sign must be highlighted same way as number. Otherwise it will be ugly.
Comment 19 André Søreng 2007-10-27 08:49:39 EDT 
1) Unsure what you mean by "p", but the patch highlights numbers that use 
exponents just fine. E.g. "10e-45" and "54.23e13" are all highlighted as
a single entity.

2) As described above, +/- in the exponent part is handled just fine.
Concerning leading +/- in numbers (e.g. "-10"), I don't think the sign
should be highlighted as part of the number, but that's probably just a matter 
of taste.

Comment 23 André Søreng 2007-10-28 09:24:32 EDT 
I added the eclipse cvs repository as a new cvs repository location:

http://wiki.eclipse.org/index.php/CVS_Howto

and then checked out the module/plugin I wanted to change.
In this case, it is "org.eclipse.jdt.ui". Just remember to pick
the version/tag/branch that roughly corresponds to the eclipse version
you are running or you might end up with some dependency problems.

Do whatever modifications to the source you want and test it with "Run/Debug as
Eclipse Application".

Unsure if there is any official tutorial, but the above works fine enough for me.

Concerning the binary exponent "p", I looked at the second edition of JLS,
and that's why I was wondering where it came from. I see now that it's only present in the third edition.
