Comment 4 Gunnar Wagenknecht 2007-08-16 01:20:51 EDT 
Other than tuning the preference to have every comment moderated we could try enabling Askimet. There are two ways to do this.

(1) Deploy it globally for all blogs with one key. (http://akismet.com/buy/non-profit/)

(2) Enable it for blogs upon request. This requires that every blog author signs up for his own key. (http://akismet.com/personal/)

I running Askimet and it's really good at filtering.
Comment 5 Kevin McGuire 2007-08-16 12:06:48 EDT 
I also run Akismet with my personal blog and have yet to see it come up with a false positive.  The amount it filters is quite frightening; I can't imaging running without it (its protected me from 1200 spam since Nov 06).  I'd be ok with us standardizing on it with a shared key for all and perhaps that would be less work for you guys.

I'd also be ok with moderating every post but it requires as much work as deleting the spam which get through (although prevention is nicer than repair).  Thus Akismet would be my preferred line of defense.

It of course would be nice to figure out if we can prevent them from circumventing the authentication process.
