Comment 13 Konstantin Komissarchik 2007-05-18 15:28:22 EDT 
Fix one deadlock; create another. :(

We've been investigating reports from our dev and qa teams that 1.5.4 is more prone to deadlocks than 1.5.3 was. We traced the problem to this fix. We need this fixed in 1.5.5 and we also need a 1.5.4 patch on the WTP update site.

Here is the analysis of the deadlock courtesy of Scott Lewis:

ModuleCore Validator

Thread acquires an object 0x74377a70 (org.eclipse.wst.common.componentcore.internal.impl.WTPModulesResource) in org.eclipse.wst.common.internal.emf.resource.TranslatorResourceImpl.load(TranslatorResourceImpl.java:388).

It then tries to get 0x802d38f0 (org.eclipse.wst.common.componentcore.internal.ModuleStructuralModel)
in org.eclipse.wst.common.internal.emfworkbench.EMFWorkbenchContext.getEditModelForRead(EMFWorkbenchContext.java:140)

and can't get it (because it's already held by other Workspace Refresh thread).

Workspace Refresh Thread

Thread acquires object 0x802d38f0 (org.eclipse.wst.common.componentcore.internal.ModuleStructuralModel) in org.eclipse.wst.common.componentcore.internal.StructureEdit.getComponentModelRoot(StructureEdit.java:450)

Then tries to get 0x74377a70 (org.eclipse.wst.common.componentcore.internal.impl.WTPModulesResource)
in org.eclipse.wst.common.internal.emf.utilities.ResourceIsLoadingAdapter.findAdapter(ResourceIsLoadingAdapter.java:36)

and can't get it because the ModuleCore Validator has got it.

So I think the story is that there is some confusion about locking in the loading/validation thread. The validation thread locks the resource and then the model, and the workspace refresh thread locks the model and tries to get the resource.

It's not obvious to me why the ModuleCore Validator thread has to lock the resource at all (in org.eclipse.wst.common.internal.emf.resource.TranslatorResourceImpl.load(TranslatorResourceImpl.java:388)

and so maybe this can be redone to not acquire this lock during load.

This should be filed as a critical bug against WST right away...unless it's already fixed in 1.5 and/or 2.0.
