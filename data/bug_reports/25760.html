Comment 2 Philipe Mulet 2002-11-06 11:34:47 EST 
The JAR you added is recorded in a classpath container, but your classpath is 
likely not using this container, but rather a variable with a related name.

You must ensure to use a container on your project classpath (instead of 
JRE_LIB which is only a variable targetting one library, as opposed to 
targetting your container).

You can do so by removing the JRE_LIB entry from your classpath, and adding 
instead a container entry (under advanced action on the library tab).

JDT/UI wants to move away from using variables since containers are more 
powerful.

Is it ok now?
