Description Anjo Krank 2006-05-02 11:12:57 EDT 
Beginning (I think) with 3.2 M5, Eclipse seems to lose the keyboard shortcut keys in some instances. I have yet to determine how exactly that happens, but from what I see, it has to do with switching to Eclipse from other applications.

This means that the markers for the keys in the main menu and context menues are gone, and pressing the shortcut keys like cmd-C and cmd-V does nothing. As you can imagine, it's a pain to not being able to copy and paste...

Deselecting the center content area and again switching from and to Eclipse makes the shortcuts appear again.
Comment 1 Anjo Krank 2006-05-03 10:27:46 EDT 
A bit more info. It happened to me 3 times today. The symtoms are:

- clicking in the menu bar shows no shortcuts
- the context menu *does* have shortcuts
- switching from and to the app alone does not help
- switching from and to the app *and* popping up the context menu *does* help
