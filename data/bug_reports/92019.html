Comment 5 Dani Megert 2005-04-20 07:12:06 EDT 
In I20050413-0910 (and older) the StyledText widget was already disposed when
SourceViewerDecorationSupport.dispose() was called and hence we did not update
the text presentation (see SourceViewerDecorationSupport.updateTextDecorations()).

In I20050413-0910 PartPane.dispose() disposed the control (StyledText widget)
before dispose was called on the part (WorkbenchPartReference.dispose()). Now in
I20050419-1200 the part is disposed before the widget.

Above comments are valid for any text-based editor, maybe even for any workbench
part.

Fixing XMLSourcePage.dispose() was a good idea in any case but I think this bug
should be reopened and moved to Platform UI to investigate whether the change
was intended. Other clients might be broken by this life-cycle change as well.

Nick please do so, if you think I'm right. Thanks.
Comment 6 Nick Edgar 2005-04-20 09:43:40 EDT 
Reopening as per Dani's suggestion.
Stefan, can you comment on the change to the ordering of dispose?
It seems very risky to change this at this point.
Comment 7 Nick Edgar 2005-04-20 09:49:40 EDT 
IWorkbenchPart.dispose() states:

     * This is the last method called on the <code>IWorkbenchPart</code>.  At this
     * point the part controls (if they were ever created) have been disposed as
part 
     * of an SWT composite.  There is no guarantee that createPartControl() has
been 
     * called, so the part controls may never have been created.

Note that the optional creation of the controls no longer occurs (since at least
3.0).  If the part is instantiated, its controls are created.
But the controls should still be disposed before the part.
